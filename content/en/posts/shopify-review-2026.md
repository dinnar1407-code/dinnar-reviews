## What's New in Shopify 2026

Shopify has been shipping fast. Here are the biggest additions:

### 1. Sidekick — Your AI Shopify Expert

Sidekick is Shopify's built-in AI assistant, available across all plans. It can:

- Write product descriptions from a photo and a few keywords
- Generate discount codes based on margin rules
- Analyze which products to restock
- Suggest theme changes ("make my store look more premium")
- Answer "how do I…" questions without Googling

In my testing, Sidekick is genuinely useful — not just a gimmick. It saves 15-30 minutes per task versus doing it manually.

### 2. Agentic Commerce (Selling in AI Channels)

This is Shopify's bet on the future. In 2026, shoppers can discover and buy your products **inside AI chat interfaces** (ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, etc.). Shopify's "Agentic" channel is free to activate — you only pay when you make a sale (2.9% + 30¢ card rate).

If AI assistants become the new Google, being listed here early is a massive distribution advantage.

### 3. Shopify Collective

A cross-store network where you can sell other brands' products without holding inventory. It's dropshipping, but with vetted Shopify merchants instead of AliExpress garbage. Margins are lower (typically 20-30%), but risk is zero.

### 4. Shopify Magic (AI Image Editor)

Generate backgrounds, remove objects, expand images, and create variants — all inside the product editor. Competes with tools like PhotoRoom and Canva's AI features, but integrated directly into your workflow.

## Key Features Deep Dive

### Store Builder

The drag-and-drop online store editor (OS 2.0) has matured significantly. Blocks-based sections let you customize every page without touching code. The 12 free themes are clean, fast, and mobile-first. Premium themes ($180-380) from the Theme Store offer more customization.

**Speed**: Shopify stores consistently score well on Core Web Vitals. In my tests, a basic store with a free theme loads in under 1.5 seconds.

### Payment Processing: Shopify Payments

If you use Shopify Payments (powered by Stripe), you skip the extra 0.5-2% transaction fees. It supports:

- Credit/debit cards (Visa, MC, Amex, Discover)
- Apple Pay, Google Pay
- Shop Pay (38% faster checkout — verified by Shopify data)
- Buy Now Pay Later (Shop Pay Installments, Affirm, Klarna)
- Local payment methods in 20+ countries

One caveat: Shopify Payments isn't available in every country. If you're in a restricted region, you'll use a third-party gateway and pay extra transaction fees.

### App Store (8,000+ Apps)

This is Shopify's real moat. The App Store has 8,000+ apps covering:

- **Email marketing**: Klaviyo, Shopify Email, Omnisend
- **Reviews**: Judge.me, Loox, Yotpo
- **SEO**: Booster SEO, Plug In SEO, Ahrefs
- **Upsells/Cross-sells**: ReConvert, Zipify, Bold Upsell
- **Shipping**: ShipStation, Shippo, Easyship
- **Accounting**: QuickBooks, Xero, A2X

Every serious store installs 6-10 apps. The ecosystem means there's almost nothing you can't do — but it also means costs add up fast.

### Shipping & Fulfillment

Shopify Shipping offers discounted rates (up to 88% off) with USPS, UPS, and DHL Express. The Shopify Fulfillment Network (SFN) handles warehousing + picking + packing — a direct competitor to Amazon FBA, but with multi-channel support.

### Shopify POS (Point of Sale)

If you sell in person, Shopify POS connects your online and offline inventory in real time. POS Lite is included with all plans. POS Pro ($89/mo/location) adds advanced staff permissions, in-store pickup management, and omnichannel features.

### International Selling: Shopify Markets

Markets lets you sell in multiple currencies, auto-translate your store, and handle local payment methods. It's included on all plans. For serious global expansion, Markets Pro handles duties, taxes, and international shipping logistics.

## Who Should Use Shopify (and Who Shouldn't)

### ✅ Shopify is perfect for:

- **First-time store owners** — the onboarding experience is polished
- **Scaling DTC brands** — handles 100 to 100,000 orders without breaking
- **Multi-channel sellers** — POS + online + social + AI channels all connected
- **Dropshippers** — Oberlo is gone, but DSers, Spocket, and Zendrop all integrate smoothly
- **Print on demand** — Printful, Printify, Gooten all have one-click integrations
- **Service businesses selling digital products** — courses, ebooks, consultations

### ❌ Shopify is not great for:

- **Pure content sites** — Shopify's blogging is an afterthought
- **Extremely custom checkout flows** — locked unless on Plus ($2,300/mo+)
- **Non-US merchants in countries without Shopify Payments** — transaction fees hurt
- **Extreme budget starters** — WooCommerce is cheaper if you're technical
